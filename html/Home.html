Preserve the Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are among the most diverse and valuable ecosystems on Earth. They support a quarter of all marine species and protect coastlines from storms and erosion.
Yet, they are under threat from climate change, pollution, and overfishing. Let's act together to protect these underwater treasures — not just for the beauty they offer, but for the balance they maintain in our oceans and the life they sustain across the planet.

Jellyfish - Nature's Drifting Wonders

Jellyfish have floated through our oceans for over 500 million years, long before the dinosaurs. Though delicate in appearance, these creatures play a vital role in marine ecosystems, serving as both predator and prey.
But climate change and pollution threaten to upset this balance. Learn more about these fascinating animals and how we can protect their fragile world.

Sea Turtles - Guardians of the Sea

Sea turtles travelthousands of kilometers across the oceans, returning to the very beaches where they were born. They help maintain healthy seagrass beds and coral reefs.
But today, they face many dangers: plastic pollution, habitat loss, and illegal hunting. Protecting sea turtles means preserving the future of our oceans.
